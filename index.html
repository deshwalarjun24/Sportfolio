The Photographer

Light is My
Native Tongue

I grew up watching my grandfather develop film in a makeshift darkroom in Lahore — the way a face would slowly emerge from nothing in that amber light left me permanently in awe of photography's alchemy.

For over nine years I have traveled across three continents, embedding myself in moments that most people rush past. My work is not about technical perfection — it is about the precise instant when authenticity and light conspire to create something irreplaceable.

Whether shooting a wedding in the Dolomites or a fashion campaign in Tokyo, I bring the same obsession: the photograph as a private conversation between subject and viewer.
